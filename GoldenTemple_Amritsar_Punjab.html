Golden Temple.

About.

The Golden Temple, also known as Sri Harmandir Sahib or Darbar Sahib, is one of the most revered religious and cultural sites in India. It is located in the city of Amritsar, in the state of Punjab, and is the central religious place for Sikhs around the world. The Golden Temple holds great significance in Sikhism and attracts millions of pilgrims and visitors every year. Architecture, The Golden Temple is a stunning example of architectural brilliance. It was originally designed by Guru Arjan Sahib, the fifth Guru of Sikhism, in the 16th century. The temple is built on a lower level, and the sanctum housing the holy scripture, Guru Granth Sahib (the holy book of Sikhism), is situated at the center of a large man-made pool called the Amrit Sarovar (Pool of Nectar). The temple's distinctive golden exterior is covered with real gold leaf, giving it a resplendent appearance that gleams in the sunligh Spiritual Significance, For Sikhs, the Golden Temple holds immense spiritual significance as it serves as the primary pilgrimage site and the most sacred Gurdwara (Sikh place of worship). The Guru Granth Sahib, which is considered the eternal Guru by Sikhs, is placed inside the sanctum and is read continuously from beginning to end. The hymns and prayers (kirtan) recited inside the temple create a serene and deeply spiritual atmospher Community Kitchen (Langar), The Golden Temple is famous for its Langar, a free community kitchen that serves meals to all visitors, regardless of their caste, creed, or religion. This concept of providing free food to everyone promotes equality, service, and the spirit of selfless giving, which are central tenets of Sikhis Festivals and Celebrations, The Golden Temple hosts several significant Sikh festivals with great enthusiasm. The most important festival is Gurupurab, which marks the birth anniversary of Guru Nanak Dev Ji, the founder of Sikhism. Thousands of devotees gather at the temple to celebrate and seek blessing Jallianwala Bagh, Adjacent to the Golden Temple complex is the Jallianwala Bagh, a historical site that commemorates the tragic massacre of unarmed civilians by British troops in 1919. The bullet marks on the walls and the Martyrs' Well are poignant reminders of the horrific event. The Golden Temple is not only a place of religious worship but also a symbol of unity, peace, and communal harmony. Visitors of all faiths are welcome to experience the spiritual ambiance, architectural grandeur, and the warm hospitality offered by the Sikh community.
